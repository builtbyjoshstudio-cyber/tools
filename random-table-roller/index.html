Your tables deserve better than a bookmark

Random tables are the great shared hobby of game masters — collected from books, blogs, forums, and 2 a.m. bursts of inspiration, then lost across screenshots and notebooks precisely when the party walks into the tavern. This tool is the machine half of the bargain: you bring the tables, it makes them rollable, findable, and permanent.

Paste anything line-shaped

Twenty plain lines become a d20 table, numbered automatically. Lines that carry their own numbers — 7: the fireplace spits green sparks — keep them, and ranges like 01-05: nothing happens become weighted entries, five times likelier than a single number. Mix all three in one paste; the parser sorts it out.

Weighted the way published tables are

The d100 tables in your favorite books lean hard on ranges — common results span ten numbers, the dragon gets exactly one. The roller respects that math: it rolls a die as big as your table's highest number and lands where the ranges say. And if your homemade table has a gap, a roll that falls in it tells you so instead of quietly rerolling — that's your cue to finish the table.

Batches without repeats

"Roll 3, no repeats" is for the moments a single result isn't enough — stocking a ruin, three rumors at the tavern, a fistful of trinkets. Distinct entries, one tap, no re-rolling the same goblin twice.

A library, not a session

Tables save themselves in your browser and stack up into a collection over time — the same quiet accumulation as the campaign journal next door. The plain-text export means nothing is trapped here: send a table you wrote to a friend, or paste it back in on another machine.

What format can I paste tables in?

Almost anything line-based. Plain lines get auto-numbered in order (twenty lines becomes a d20 table). Lines that start with a number ('7: a broken cart') keep that number, and ranges ('01-05: nothing happens') become weighted entries — the wider the range, the more likely the result. You can mix all three styles in one paste.

How do weighted entries work?

The roller rolls a die as big as your table's highest number and finds the entry whose range covers the roll. An entry spanning 1-5 is five times likelier than one covering a single number. If your ranges leave gaps, a roll landing in a gap says so honestly instead of pretending.

Where are my tables stored, and can I share them?

Tables live in your browser's local storage — no account, nothing uploaded. The export button turns a table into plain numbered text you can save, send to a friend, or paste right back in on another device.

Is this table roller free?

Yes — free, no ads, no signup, and it runs entirely in your browser. Your tables stay on your own device.
