Chef's Tip for Fractional Eggs: If your scaled recipe calls for a fraction of an egg, whisk a whole egg in a small bowl. 1 whole egg equals roughly 3 Tablespoons (or 46g). For 0.5 eggs, use exactly 1.5 Tablespoons. For smaller fractions, use a single egg yolk or a splash of milk.
Chef's Note: Volumetric baking measurements (cups) become highly inaccurate when scaled up massively due to compaction. For bulletproof professional results, consider converting your core ingredients to weight (grams).

Chef's Corner: Mastering the Pan Sauce

  • Watch the Reduction: Scaling a pan sauce means more liquid to boil off. Keep a close eye on the reduction consistency rather than just relying on a timer.
  • Deglaze for Depth: When scaling up, ensure you’re still getting all the flavor from the pan. Using wine or spirits to deglaze the 'fond' is the key to a professional sauce.
  • The Fraction Factor: This tool handles the math, but remember that some ingredients—like salt or high-heat spices—might not need a perfect 1:1 scale-up. Taste as you go!
  • Monter au Beurre: For a mirror-like shine and velvety texture, whisk in chilled cubes of unsalted butter only after pulling the pan from the heat.
  • The Acid Correction: If a reduction feels heavy or flat, it’s usually missing acid, not salt. A few drops of fresh lemon juice or sherry vinegar at the end will brighten the flavors.
  • The 'Noir' Finish: For a truly professional, silky presentation, pour the finished sauce through a fine-mesh strainer (chinois) to remove spent shallots and herb stems.
